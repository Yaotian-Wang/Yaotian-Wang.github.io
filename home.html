Welcome to Yaotian Wang's personal website!

I am a final-year Ph.D. candidate at the Department of Statistics, University of Pittsburgh. My research interests are in the multidisciplinary areas of human brain mapping and computational neuroscience. My overarching goals are to provide a comprehensive understanding of how the brain's functional organization brings about different brain functions and how it differs in psychiatric and neurological illnesses. Specifically, I develop efficient and robust statistical modeling methods to analyze massive brain data and study the functional organization of normal and abnormal human brains. The statistical methods that I have developed are in three major statistical fields: Bayesian inference, network analysis, and high-dimensional data analysis.
